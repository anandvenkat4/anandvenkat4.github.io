Overview

What this course does

You'll learn to craft effective prompts for AI models and to evaluate how well they perform — by building. Roughly 60% of contact time is spent writing, testing, and measuring prompts across three platforms, so you leave with skills, not just notes.

Build, don't just read

Eight guided labs and a capstone. Every technique is practiced on a real task before moving on.

Grounded in research

Content follows The Prompt Report taxonomy and best practice from OpenAI, Anthropic, and Vanderbilt's prompt-pattern work.

Zero-cost tooling

Labs run on free web tools and local open-source models (Ollama). No paid API keys required.

On successful completion of the course, students will be able to:

Assessment

How you're graded

Balanced across in-class labs, a take-home assignment, and a capstone demo/viva — mapped to CO1 and CO2, weighted toward Bloom's Understand and Apply levels per the approved pattern.

40% · Labs

Completion and quality of the eight in-class labs. Continuous, formative.

35% · Take-home assignment

Build-and-evaluate a prompt-based application with a short technical report.

25% · Capstone + viva

Team demo of a prompt-based app plus a short individual viva.

CO1

Design & optimize

Assessed through Labs 0–7, the patterns toolkit, and the assignment build.

CO2

Assess & evaluate

Assessed through Lab 8 (security + eval), the benchmarking case study, and the capstone report.
